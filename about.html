Welcome to My Corner of the Threat Landscape

I’m kum1h0x, a Senior Security Researcher specializing in reverse-engineering APT malware, sandbox internals, and threat detection — with public research presented at AVAR, Offzone, and SOC Forum.

My journey in cybersecurity began at Positive Technologies, where I worked on malware reverse engineering and detection logic — writing YARA rules, behavioral rules for PT Sandbox, and a Drakvuf sandbox plugin. I then moved to Kaspersky Lab, focusing on exploit reverse engineering, detection rule development, vulnerability reproduction, in-the-wild exploit monitoring, and honeypot development.

That foundation propelled me into a threat research role at Solar 4RAYS (Rostelecom-Solar), where I spent close to two years performing in-depth reverse engineering of APT malware and authoring public technical reports. I’ve shared this research at major conferences including SOC Forum, Offzone, and AVAR, most notably on IDFKA — a multi-feature Rust-based backdoor attributed to NGC5081 that supports a dozen operating modes and uses a custom Layer-4 protocol, eBPF/ptrace-based stealth techniques, and environment-gated decryption to evade analysis.

You may also know my work from the “Shedding Zmiy” research series, where I traced a highly adaptive Eastern European APT group and dissected the source code of their Bulldog Backdoor web panel — obtained thanks to an OPSEC mistake on the attackers’ side — later discussed on the Risky Biz podcast. Other research along the way ranged from DDoS tooling (Gorgon Stress) and Android spyware (ClayRat) to C2 pivoting techniques used by Erudite Mogwai, picking up coverage from The Hacker News, Forbes, and RBC.

Today, I’m a Senior Malware Analyst at BI.ZONE, working on sandbox development and building detection ranging from static signatures to full behavioral analytics. This blog is where I’ll publish deep dives into:

  • Malware reverse engineering (with a focus on Rust, .NET, and native implants)
  • Threat hunting methodologies and detection rule architecture (YARA, Sigma, EDR telemetry)
  • APT campaign attribution and TTP mapping
  • Hard-won lessons from incident response and sandbox evasion techniques
